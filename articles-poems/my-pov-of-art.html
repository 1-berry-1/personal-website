I think it is pretty clear that art is a form of expression. But it is also quite hard to feel the weight behind cliche words. Any form of art - be it on paper, digitally, with different medium, through wool and thread, pottery, clay, jewellery, photography. All is some medium of expressing what's inside. What is art exactly? I think it is expressing the human mind, as it is, through all the chaos, but in a controlled and precise manner. Science also does a good job at applying our brains to study the world, but art feels original. Art feels human.

I am still not sure how can I describe art in a way that is understandable to people.

There are three aspects to making good art, from my observation so far in my journey

  1. Control over your hand. For art in general, this could be mastery over the basics
  2. Replication, either through observation, or the imagination
  3. The art of art. What makes poems, what makes Shakespeare famous, what makes movies like Titanic
