I recently watched an episode of 'The Mentalist' where a man was made to believe that a bag with dead bodies was a bag of potatoes that needed to be delivered to the police.

That made me think, about all the times when people are like, if you're saying the truth look me in the eyes. I've run the simulation a couple times in my head for a secret that I had hidden from my parents and, I could do it, through making myself believe in whatever I was saying. I lied to them in actuality too, with complete confidence, I saw the posture and facial expressions of my mum relaxing as I went through my totally made up story. She came to the point that scared her the most, and I flawlessly fooled her. How?

Is lying that easy?
Do we fumble at a lie detector because it is supposed to be 'all-knowing' and we're afraid of being caught? Maybe believing in the lie itself will make you an efficient liar?

I was right.

Polygraph is pseudo-scientific.
It only measures your vitals, a make-believe for the patient.

Lying might be related to prefrontal activity, so can we consider lying intensively to be a default feature of human nature? (people with Parkinson's find it hard to lie)
If you think about it, things like, gossiping, lying, indulging in desires, sexuality, are all considered 'taboo' in most religions, yet, they seem to be something very inherent to being human.

What is the boundary between a truth and a lie? Because we are living in, in the words of Yuval, imagined realities. Elaborate lies that all of us believe in, institutions and gods. Yet, lying about everyday stuff, is a lie. But money, is not considered a lie, when clearly it doesn't hold as much value as everyone makes it out to be. What?
