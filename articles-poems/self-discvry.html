Lesson from my self discovery

  1. It's not you against the world, it's you in your own world.
  2. Jumping jacks/holding ice against the nape of your neck/grounding yourself with the 54321 technique/ is go to to reset your nervous system, when you’re running wild. Slows you down to think.
  3. It’s okay to fail after trying something out, and to dislike something when you try it. Don’t have to force or restrain yourself from trying. (why? speaking from experience, I always got something out of every situation. Even travelling itself, seems so pointless and boring, there's no clear thing that you can point out that this is a win. It's always the experience that matters more to you, what will you do if you win? Win at the price of yourself? Is that worth it?)
  4. Break things down into very simple small steps, any task you take up. Ensures you stay in the present, and not jump steps. One thing at a time is the best strategy. All of this overwhelms you and shuts you down. It’s unnecessary.
  5. Always remember positive reinforcement. Bringing yourself down to motivate you to do things is not going to help with your motivation. At least for the sake of that, but in reality you are worth every bit of that appreciation.
  6. Being just you is enough, no matter how silly or introverted you are. You don’t have to be someone else to be liked and invited. You are going to find the right people like this by being you, and not people like srinidhi and harshita.
  7. Disagreement and disapproval are only opinions. You pave who you become and what you do, no one else. There will be someone who agrees with you!
  8. It’s okay to make mistakes and blunders, to get hurt, to be slow, to say things you don’t mean, to be dynamic, to go with the flow, to have a void mind, to open up to people. All of it is part of being human, you don’t have to prove anything to anyone
  9. You have the right to have opinions, just as everyone else does. You can disagree to opinions, just as anyone else can disagree with yours. You matter just as everyone else matters.
  10. Black and whites. Should and musts. These are common patterns one can fall into, which do not reflect reality.
  11. When socializing, are you having fun? Are you being in the present? Taking it step by step?
